Project Idea

Overview:

The idea is a mobile app used for productivity and organisation. A habit tracker of sorts, or to-do list, however the twist is that it has game elements or attributes that make the user more motivated to complete their set tasks. For example giving awards or achievements to users who complete a number of set tasks in a day or week. And as users continue to complete their tasks more features of the app would be unlocked.

Motivation:

Teenage and young adult productivity isn’t the highest. Piers Steel, a business professor at the University of Calgary, noted in a 2007 study. “Estimates indicate that 80 to 95 percent of college students engage in procrastination. The hope is that the app will allow students and others to be more productive in their day-to-day life, having a reward for completing a task will help students to procrastinate less and work in hopes of receiving said reward.

Skills Required:

Will require extensive programming knowledge, most likely in Java (most used programming language in app building).

Outcome:

The outcome would be that the app would help students and young adults be more productive in completing their tasks. Helping fix the problem of procrastination and unproductively in people’s day to day lives. The impact may not the hugest but would still help many people find a way to be more organised with their lives, and manage their time better. It would help impact some student’s school lives and personal lives.
